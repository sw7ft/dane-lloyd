## OFFICIAL BIO (Verbatim from danelloyd.ca)

> Dane grew up in and around the riding of Parkland. His family has farmed outside of Spruce Grove for generations, and he is a proud life-long Albertan. Dane, his wife Raechel and their three children Brooke, Alexander, and Harrison split their time between their home in Stony Plain and Ottawa. Dane graduated from Edmonton Christian High School in 2009 and pursued a degree in history and political studies at Trinity Western University where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in 2014. Before serving as a member of Parliament, he worked as a Parliamentary Advisor to St. Albert-Edmonton MP Michael Cooper and the Honourable Ed Fast who served as the Minister of International Trade.
>
> Dane is currently the Shadow Minister for Emergency Preparedness and Community Resilience, responsible for holding the government accountable for its management of disasters and emergencies. In July 2024, Albertans were devastated by the catastrophic wildfire in Jasper. As the Shadow Minister, Dane worked tirelessly to press the Liberal government for answers on how the fire was allowed to happen. The subsequent Parliamentary inquiry revealed that the Liberal government knew Jasper was in danger for years, but failed to act to remove dry, pine beetle-infested deadwood.
>
> On top of his role in the Shadow Cabinet, Dane is a sitting member on the Standing Committee on Public Safety and National Security where he has fought hard against the Liberal gun confiscation regime which targeted responsible firearms owners including hunters and sports shooters. He was also at the forefront of the battle against foreign interference in our elections and improving Canada's cybersecurity legislation to ensure that our society is better prepared to meet these threats. In addition to his political resume, Dane holds a commission as a Captain in the Canadian Armed Forces Primary Reserve.

## KEY POLICY POSITIONS & ACHIEVEMENTS

### McCann's Law — Private Member's Bill (Bill C-236)
- Introduced September 22, 2025 — the "Addressing the Continuing Victimization of Homicide Victims' Families Act"
- Named after **Lyle and Marie McCann**, an elderly Alberta couple murdered in 2010 whose remains were never found
- **Travis Vader** was convicted of manslaughter in their deaths but has refused to disclose the location of their remains
- Amends the Criminal Code, Corrections and Conditional Release Act, and Prisons and Reformatories Act
- Makes refusal to disclose the location of victims' remains an **aggravating sentencing factor**
- Delays parole eligibility for offenders who refuse to cooperate
- Similar to "no body, no parole" laws in Australia and the UK
- Dane introduced versions in **every Parliament**: Bill C-437 (42nd), Bill C-316 (43rd), Bill C-424 (44th), Bill C-236 (45th)
- Campaigned on this promise in his very first 2017 by-election — delivered the legislation eight years later
- Dane: *"Families deserve the right to know where their loved ones are and the right to give them a proper funeral."*

### Jasper Wildfire Response (2024)
- As Shadow Minister, pressed the government for answers after the catastrophic Jasper wildfire in July 2024
- Parliamentary hearings revealed Parks Canada had "seven years in which to do something" but failed to act
- Called for long-term planning and better coordination between municipal, provincial, and federal fire agencies
- Advocates for a national fire administration

### Firearms & Hunting Rights
- Fought against Bill C-21 (Liberal gun confiscation regime) targeting responsible firearms owners, hunters, and sports shooters
- Exposed that no new consultations were conducted before major amendments to Bill C-21 were introduced
- Raised concerns about the government's failure to consult First Nations as required under UNDRIP
- Defends the rights of Canada's 2.3 million lawful firearms owners

### Emergencies Act Opposition (2022)
- Opposed the Liberal government's unprecedented invocation of the Emergencies Act (February 2022)
- Quoted: *"Parliamentarians deserve real evidence, not conjecture, from this minister before we can ever contemplate suspending the rights of Canadians."*
- The invocation was later found **unjustified** by the Federal Court — vindicating Dane's position

### Energy & Pipelines
- Strong advocate for Alberta's energy sector and pipeline development
- Spoke in the House (March 2026): *"The world is facing the biggest energy crisis since the 1973 oil embargo. Millions of barrels have been taken off-line, and Canada is one of the countries best positioned to respond."*
- Defends the livelihoods of energy workers across Parkland riding, particularly in Drayton Valley and Brazeau County

### Veterans & Military Families
- Served on the **Standing Committee on Veterans Affairs** (43rd Parliament, February–August 2020)
- Moved a motion calling for an investigation into Veterans Affairs restricting mental health services for families of injured veterans
- Quoted: *"As someone who has served in the Forces, we all know that if somebody serves, their family serves with them."*

### Foreign Interference & Cybersecurity
- At the forefront of the battle against foreign interference in Canadian elections
- Active on Bill C-8 (cybersecurity), pushing for judicial oversight before the government can compel action from telecom companies
- Advocated for "front-end" judicial authorization rather than only after-the-fact review

### Auto Theft & Organized Crime
- Highlighted Canada's auto theft crisis — at one point in 2022, a vehicle was being stolen every 5 minutes
- Argued Canada has "become a massive exporter of stolen cars and a net importer of illegal firearms" under the Liberal government

### Bail & Sentencing Reform
- Supported Bill C-14, the Bail and Sentencing Reform Act
- Raised a constituent case in **Spring Meadows Estates** (near Edmonton) where a neighbour's house was shot up twice in three weeks by extortionists: *"We live in fear, don't go out after dark."*

### Indigenous Advocacy — The Michel Band
- Advocates in Parliament for the **Michel band**, an Iroquois First Nation in his riding
- The Michel band was **the only entire First Nation ever enfranchised in Canada** — forcibly dissolved in 1958
- Called for federal legislation to address this historic injustice
- Supported Bill C-10 (Commissioner for Modern Treaty Implementation Act)

### Cost of Living & the Economy
- Quoted (February 2026): *"Liberal spin cannot avoid the facts. Under the Prime Minister, the rich are getting richer while working Canadians are falling further and further behind."*
- Advocates for removing hidden taxes that make food more expensive

### Ukrainian Heritage Month (Bill S-210)
- Spoke in the House (February 2026) in support of Bill S-210, establishing September as Ukrainian Heritage Month
- Bill passed unanimously (318 Yea, 0 Nay)

### Member's Statements & Tributes
- **Glenn Hall tribute (February 2026)** — Honoured the passing of legendary goaltender Glenn Hall, calling him "a true prairie boy"
- **Humboldt Broncos tribute (April 2018)** — Delivered emotional tribute for Conner Lukan and Parker Tobin, two players from his riding killed in the bus crash. Wore a hockey jersey in Parliament alongside MPs from all parties.

## SUMMARY

Dane Lloyd is a fourth-generation Albertan who has represented Parkland riding since 2017 — re-elected four times, most recently in April 2025 with **75.2% of the vote and 53,468 ballots cast**. He combines deep Parliamentary expertise with genuine community roots to fight for Parkland families on the issues that matter most.

His portfolio is remarkably broad: emergency preparedness (Jasper wildfire), public safety (auto theft, extortion, organized crime), firearms rights (Bill C-21), cybersecurity (Bill C-8), victims' justice (McCann's Law), veterans' mental health, Indigenous reconciliation (Michel band), energy exports, cost of living, and foreign interference.

McCann's Law — a "no body, no parole" promise made in his very first 2017 campaign and delivered as Bill C-236 in 2025 — exemplifies his commitment to following through. He introduced a version of that bill in every single Parliament he has served in (4 times across 8 years).

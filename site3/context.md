## Official Bio (Verbatim from danelloyd.ca)

> Dane grew up in and around the riding of Parkland. His family has farmed outside of Spruce Grove for generations, and he is a proud life-long Albertan. Dane, his wife Raechel and their three children Brooke, Alexander, and Harrison split their time between their home in Stony Plain and Ottawa. Dane graduated from Edmonton Christian High School in 2009 and pursued a degree in history and political studies at Trinity Western University where he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in 2014. Before serving as a member of Parliament, he worked as a Parliamentary Advisor to St. Albert-Edmonton MP Michael Cooper and the Honourable Ed Fast who served as the Minister of International Trade.
>
> Dane is currently the Shadow Minister for Emergency Preparedness and Community Resilience, responsible for holding the government accountable for its management of disasters and emergencies. In July 2024, Albertans were devastated by the catastrophic wildfire in Jasper. As the Shadow Minister, Dane worked tirelessly to press the Liberal government for answers on how the fire was allowed to happen. The subsequent Parliamentary inquiry revealed that the Liberal government knew Jasper was in danger for years, but failed to act to remove dry, pine beetle-infested deadwood.
>
> On top of his role in the Shadow Cabinet, Dane is a sitting member on the Standing Committee on Public Safety and National Security where he has fought hard against the Liberal gun confiscation regime which targeted responsible firearms owners including hunters and sports shooters. He was also at the forefront of the battle against foreign interference in our elections and improving Canada's cybersecurity legislation to ensure that our society is better prepared to meet these threats. In addition to his political resume, Dane holds a commission as a Captain in the Canadian Armed Forces Primary Reserve.

## Key Policy Positions & Achievements

### Firearms & Hunting Rights
- Fought hard against the Liberal gun confiscation regime (Bill C-21) that targeted responsible firearms owners, hunters, and sports shooters
- Exposed that no new consultations were conducted before major amendments to Bill C-21 were introduced
- Raised concerns about the government's failure to consult First Nations as required under UNDRIP
- Defends the rights of Canada's 2.3 million lawful firearms owners
- Advocates for focusing enforcement on smuggling and gang violence rather than penalizing legal gun owners

### Jasper Wildfire Response (2024)
- As Shadow Minister, worked tirelessly to press the government for answers after the catastrophic Jasper wildfire in July 2024
- Participated in parliamentary hearings that revealed Parks Canada and the federal government had "seven years in which to do something" but failed to implement wildfire mitigation recommendations
- Called for long-term planning and better coordination between municipal, provincial, and federal fire agencies
- Advocates for a national fire administration

### Foreign Interference & Cybersecurity
- At the forefront of the battle against foreign interference in Canadian elections
- Worked on improving Canada's cybersecurity legislation
- Vocal advocate for protecting the integrity of democratic institutions

### Emergencies Act Opposition (2022)
- Opposed the Liberal government's unprecedented invocation of the Emergencies Act in February 2022 — the first time since the Act's passage in 1988
- Quoted (February 17, 2022): *"Parliamentarians deserve real evidence, not conjecture, from this minister before we can ever contemplate suspending the rights of Canadians."*
- Challenged the government to substantiate claims about "ideologically motivated violent extremism" ties with concrete evidence
- The invocation was later found to have been **unjustified** by the Federal Court — vindicating Dane's demand for evidence

### McCann's Law — Private Member's Bill (Bill C-236)
- Introduced September 22, 2025 — the "Addressing the Continuing Victimization of Homicide Victims' Families Act"
- Known as **McCann's Law**, named after Lyle and Marie McCann, an elderly Alberta couple murdered in 2010 whose remains were never found
- **Travis Vader** was convicted of manslaughter in their deaths and sentenced to life in prison, but has refused to disclose the location of their remains
- Amends the Criminal Code, Corrections and Conditional Release Act, and Prisons and Reformatories Act
- Makes refusal to disclose the location of victims' remains an **aggravating sentencing factor**
- Delays parole eligibility for offenders who refuse to cooperate
- Empowers judges, parole boards, and correctional officers to hold offenders accountable
- Similar to "no body, no parole" laws that already exist in Australia and the UK
- Originally campaigned on "no body, no parole" during his 2017 by-election — followed through with legislation eight years later
- Dane has stated that families deserve "the right to know where their loved ones are" and "the right to give them a proper funeral"
- Dane introduced versions of this bill in **every Parliament** he has served in: Bill C-437 (42nd), Bill C-316 (43rd), Bill C-424 (44th), and Bill C-236 (45th) — demonstrating relentless persistence
- As of September 2025, the bill was at second reading in the House of Commons

### Auto Theft & Organized Crime
- Highlighted Canada's auto theft crisis — at one point in 2022, a vehicle was being stolen every 5 minutes
- Emphasized that auto theft funds organized crime, international terrorism, and illegal firearms purchases
- Argued Canada has "become a massive exporter of stolen cars and a net importer of illegal firearms" under the Liberal government

### Energy & Pipelines
- Strong advocate for Alberta's energy sector and pipeline development
- Spoken at the Natural Resources committee on Canadian Energy Exports — pushed witnesses on how to make Canada a more attractive destination for energy investment
- Has "stood up for the interests of the region, whether it is advocating for pipelines or jobs in the energy and other sectors"
- Defends the livelihoods of energy workers across Parkland riding, particularly in Drayton Valley and Brazeau County
- Spoke in the House (March 2026): "The world is facing the biggest energy crisis since the 1973 oil embargo. Millions of barrels have been taken off-line, and Canada is one [of the countries best positioned to respond]"
- Advocates for responsible resource development that supports Canadian families while meeting global energy demand

### Veterans & Military Families
- Served on the **Standing Committee on Veterans Affairs** (43rd Parliament, February–August 2020)
- Moved a motion calling for an investigation into Veterans Affairs restricting mental health services for families of injured veterans
- Quoted: *"As someone who has served in the Forces, we all know that if somebody serves, their family serves with them. When I read reports that families are being denied previously-approved mental health services, I knew this was an issue we needed to bring up right away."*
- Called for testimony from Veterans Ombudsman Craig Dalton and senior officials to bring transparency to the department's policies
- Pushed for the Minister to issue clear direction to protect veterans' families' access to counselling

### Cybersecurity & Critical Infrastructure (Bill C-8)
- Active on the **Public Safety Committee** during study of Bill C-8 (An Act respecting cyber security)
- Challenged government witnesses on the bill's grant of sweeping executive powers without prior judicial authorization
- Argued that the government already has cybersecurity capabilities and questioned whether new powers are being adequately checked
- Advocated for "front-end" judicial authorization before the government compels action from telecom companies, rather than only after-the-fact review
- Part of the Conservative push for proper parliamentary and judicial oversight of government cybersecurity powers

### Bail & Sentencing Reform
- Supported Bill C-14, the Bail and Sentencing Reform Act
- Spoke in the House (November 2025) about rising extortion and violent crime affecting his constituents
- Highlighted a constituent in **Spring Meadows Estates** (near Edmonton) whose neighbour's house was shot up twice in three weeks by extortionists: *"We live in fear, don't go out after dark"*
- Criticized Bill C-75 (2019) for directing courts to release accused persons "at the earliest opportunity, with the least onerous conditions"
- Criticized Bill C-5 (2022) for repealing mandatory minimum sentences for firearm offences and extortion involving firearms, and for allowing house arrest for serious offences
- Voted in favour of reversing these changes; the Liberal government voted against

### Indigenous Advocacy — The Michel Band
- Spoke in the House of Commons (October 2023) about the **Michel band**, an Iroquois First Nation in his riding
- Highlighted that the Michel band was **the only entire First Nation ever enfranchised in Canada** — forcibly dissolved in 1958
- While 700 members regained individual Indian status under Bill C-31 in the 1980s, the band itself remains unrecognized and unable to make land claims
- Called for federal legislation to address this historic injustice
- Supported Bill C-10 (Commissioner for Modern Treaty Implementation Act), speaking in the House (February 2026) about the importance of honouring modern treaties

### Ukrainian Heritage Month (Bill S-210)
- Spoke in the House (February 2026) in support of Bill S-210, establishing September as Ukrainian Heritage Month
- The bill recognizes Canada's ~1.3 million Ukrainian-Canadians and their contributions to Canadian society
- Bill passed unanimously (318 Yea, 0 Nay)

### RCMP & Rural Policing
- Advocate for maintaining RCMP contract policing in rural Alberta communities
- Spoke on the Public Complaints and Review Commission Act, supporting accountability while defending the work of law enforcement officers
- Stated that officers make "incredible sacrifices" and that complaints must be handled "in a timely and efficient manner"
- Concerned about impacts on rural communities that rely entirely on RCMP for law enforcement

### Cost of Living & the Economy
- Vocal critic of government economic policy on behalf of working Canadians
- Spoke in the House (February 2026): *"Liberal spin cannot avoid the facts. Under the Prime Minister, the rich are getting richer while working Canadians are falling further and further behind."*
- Advocates for removing hidden taxes that make food more expensive
- Supports policies to encourage investment in Canadian businesses and resource development

### Foreign Nationals & Border Security
- Participated in the Public Safety Committee study on **Canada's Ability to Remove Foreign Nationals with a Criminal Record** (December 2025)
- Raised concerns about visa requirement changes made by the government and their impact on public safety
- Advocates for a system that efficiently removes foreign nationals who pose a threat to Canadians

## Summary

Dane Lloyd is a fourth-generation Albertan who has represented Parkland riding since 2017 — re-elected four times, most recently in April 2025 with 75.2% of the vote and 53,468 ballots cast. He combines deep Parliamentary expertise with genuine community roots to fight for Parkland families on the issues that matter most.

His portfolio is remarkably broad for a backbench/shadow cabinet MP: emergency preparedness (Jasper wildfire), public safety (auto theft, extortion, organized crime), firearms rights (Bill C-21), cybersecurity (Bill C-8), victims' justice (McCann's Law), veterans' mental health, Indigenous reconciliation (Michel band), energy exports, cost of living, and foreign interference. He raises real constituent stories on the House floor — from the Spring Meadows extortion crisis to the Humboldt Broncos victims from his riding.

McCann's Law — a "no body, no parole" promise made in his very first 2017 campaign and delivered as Bill C-236 in 2025 — exemplifies his commitment to following through. He introduced a version of that bill in every single Parliament he has served in (4 times across 8 years). Dane Lloyd doesn't just talk about issues; he brings legislation, moves committee motions, and holds the government accountable with evidence and persistence.
